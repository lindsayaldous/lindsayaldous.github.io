One task I completed this summer was updating the Kincardine Trails Association's master trail map. The previous version had been done by another summer student in 2019, but they used Adobe Illustrator and left no map document behind. The municipality didn't have a license to use Illustrator, so I had to recreate the entire map in ArcMap, mimicking the design choices as closely as possible because we could not verify why they had been selected in the first place - were they made at the discretion of the previous map author? Or were they requested by the trails association?

There were a few changes that had to be made to the original. There was a new Biking Loop to create, the head of the trails association requested the trail point labels to all be kept but to be made smaller, and the road labels badly needed halos to be more legible

could also add the station beach parking map I was asked to make? It was a map that showed the locations of new parking signs and routes at the main town beach. The map was requested by a clerk for their report to council on the topic, and was kept very, very simple. They wanted the aerial photo for context, and very simple coloured markers to outline the changes for the parking by-law.
